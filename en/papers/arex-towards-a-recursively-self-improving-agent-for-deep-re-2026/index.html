Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Recursively Self-Improving (RSI) agent architecture alternating outer self-improvement loop (auditing, identifying unresolved claims, launching targeted follow-up) with inner research loop
  • Autonomous context-update tool that compresses growing interaction history into a compact improvement state preserving verified evidence and unresolved constraints, without external model
  • Training via agentic mid-training and long-horizon reinforcement learning with emphasis on key steps where decisive evidence is acquired or errors are corrected, mitigating sparse final rewards
  • Instantiation of dense 4B and 122B-A10B Mixture-of-Experts models achieving strong performance

Methodology

AREX alternates between an inner research loop that gathers evidence and constructs a provisional answer, and an outer self-improvement loop that audits the answer constraint-wise, identifies unresolved claims, and launches targeted follow-up research. It learns an autonomous context-update tool to compress history into a compact improvement state. Training uses verified synthetic tasks and high-quality trajectories through agentic mid-training and long-horizon RL, with emphasis on key steps. Evaluated against comparable-scale baselines on BrowseComp, WideSearch, DeepSearchQA, HLE, and other reasoning/tool-use benchmarks.

Key Results

AREX substantially outperforms comparable-scale baselines and remains competitive with models using substantially more activated parameters across deep research and reasoning benchmarks.
