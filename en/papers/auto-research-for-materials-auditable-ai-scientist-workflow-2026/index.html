Auto Research for Materials: Auditable AI-Scientist Workflows with Held-Out Transfer

arXiv 2026 65.3 method, system, application

TLDR

An AI research agent for materials science performs automated searches over modeling changes and validates them on held-out data, achieving significant improvements.

Reasoning

Strengths: rigorous held-out validation, large number of experiments, clear separation of change types. Weaknesses: limited to specific materials benchmarks, may not generalize to other domains; no discussion of computational cost or scalability.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Held-out transfer evaluation design that tests whether AI-scientist decisions survive unseen data and produce reusable code.
  • Separation of the search space into feature, model, representation, and training data changes, with agents using only the mean over five inner folds to avoid reliance on a single development split.
  • Demonstration that separately discovered changes can be combined across tasks and reused, revealing two distinct materials modelling regimes (composition-based vs. structure-based).
  • Evidence that closed-loop agents can produce executable discoveries that survive beyond the feedback loop.

Methodology

The search space is split into changes to features, models, representations, and training data. Seven searches produce 701 evaluated changes across ten Matbench endpoints, with agents receiving only the mean over five inner folds as feedback. The selected code is then frozen and evaluated once on an untouched holdout set.

Key Results

Nine of ten selected interventions remained the best tested single change. For composition tasks, held-out MAE reductions of 17.4% (band gap) and 18.6% (steel strength) and gains on both classification endpoints were achieved, while structure tasks benefited from geometric descriptors and model/calibration changes (mean MAE reductions of 14.6% and 7.1%). Combining separately found feature and model changes yielded a 26.3% mean held-out improvement.

Limitations

  • One of the ten selected interventions did not remain the best on the holdout, indicating imperfect robustness.
  • External data screening added little for composition tasks, suggesting limited benefit from that search dimension.
  • Evaluation is limited to ten Matbench endpoints; generalizability to other materials domains is not demonstrated.
