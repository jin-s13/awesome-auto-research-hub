Are Large Language Models Reliable AI Scientists? Assessing Reverse-Engineering of Black-Box Systems

arXiv 2025 62.8 method

TLDR

LLMs fail to reverse-engineer black-box systems from passive observation but improve with active intervention, though still fall short of Bayesian inference.

Reasoning

The paper provides a systematic evaluation of LLMs as reverse-engineering agents, highlighting the benefit of active intervention and identifying failure modes like overcomplication. However, the experiments are limited to synthetic black-box systems, and the performance plateau suggests significant limitations for real-world scientific discovery.

Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Prompting LLMs to actively intervene (query) black-box systems instead of only passively observing, improving reverse-engineering performance.
  • Identification of two common failure modes—overcomplication and overlooking—that intervention helps LLMs escape.
  • Demonstration that the benefit of intervention stems partly from the process of generating effective queries, not just the resulting data.

Methodology

The study evaluates LLMs on reverse-engineering three types of black-box systems (Program, Formal Language, Math Equation) under passive observation and active intervention conditions, comparing performance to Bayesian inference and analyzing failure modes.

Key Results

LLMs plateau below Bayesian inference with passive observation, but active intervention improves performance by enabling edge-case testing and belief refinement; intervention helps escape overcomplication and overlooking failures.

Limitations

  • LLMs still fall short of the Bayesian inference ideal even with intervention.
  • The performance improvement from intervention is only partial.
  • The investigation is limited to three specific black-box system types.
