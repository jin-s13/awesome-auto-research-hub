Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Formulation of automated technological roadmapping as extracting scientific contributions and linking them to prerequisites
  • Creation of the Scientific Contribution Graph, a large-scale resource with 2 million contributions and 12.5 million prerequisite edges from 230k papers
  • Introduction of the scientific prerequisite prediction task to forecast enabling technologies for future discoveries
  • Demonstration that contemporary models achieve 0.48 MAP on this task using temporally filtered backtesting

Methodology

The authors extracted scientific contributions from 230k open-access AI/NLP papers and constructed a graph linking contributions to their prerequisites, resulting in 2 million nodes and 12.5 million edges. They then framed a prediction task where models predict prerequisite relationships and evaluated them with temporally filtered backtesting.

Key Results

The Scientific Contribution Graph comprises 2 million contributions and 12.5 million prerequisite edges; models reach 0.48 mean average precision on the scientific prerequisite prediction task.
