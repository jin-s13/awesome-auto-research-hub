Deep Analysis

Innovations

  • Application of Multi-Objective Bayesian Optimization (MOBO) to balance multiple competing rewards in autonomous scanning probe microscopy imaging.
  • Use of the Pareto front to not only guide optimization but also extract physical insights into trade-offs between measurement quality, reproducibility, and efficiency.
  • Integration of human-in-the-loop decision-making within the MOBO framework, allowing researchers to fine-tune experimental trade-offs based on domain expertise.

Methodology

Multi-Objective Bayesian Optimization is applied to optimize scanning probe microscopy imaging parameters. The approach balances three objectives—measurement quality, reproducibility, and efficiency—by computing and analyzing the Pareto front. The framework also supports human-in-the-loop interaction for adjusting trade-offs.

Key Results

MOBO successfully optimizes SPM imaging parameters, enhancing measurement quality, reproducibility, and efficiency. The Pareto front reveals physical insights into the trade-offs among these objectives.
